How it works

A New Era of the World Cup

🌍

48 Teams, 12 Groups

The field expands from 32 to 48 nations split into 12 groups of four. The top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed teams advance.

🆕

New Round of 32

For the first time a 32-team knockout round follows the groups — more do-or-die drama on the road to the final than ever before.

🏟️

16 Iconic Cities

From the historic Estadio Azteca to LA's futuristic SoFi Stadium, three nations open their greatest venues across four time zones.

⚽

104 Matches, 39 Days

The most matches in World Cup history, climaxing on July 19, 2026 at MetLife Stadium in New York/New Jersey.
